My Best Work

Reflection:

It was honestly difficult to pick this project as my best work because out of all the projects that actually required coding and actually took so much “sweat and tears”, I decided to choose a google slideshow presentation.

I was choosing between the digital prototype project, the “design a game” project, and this slideshow project. The reason I chose this as my best work is because I feel that there’s not as many things I “should’ve added” compared to the coding projects which, although made with full creativity and dedication, had several components that I could’ve added that would've made it my “best” work. But aside from that, this is an example of my best work because I feel it fully represents my devotion to making slideshows informational, yet aesthetically appealing. It also shows my creativity in designing an app, and all the problems it can solve. I am fully impressed that I was capable of brainstorming this app idea with a very aesthetic slideshow and thought process. I found this work to be easy, though VERY time-consuming, especially since this was my first comp sci project, and, as the busy procrastinator as I am, it was definitely a punch in the gut to realize that I should’ve planned my project process sooner and more efficiently, rather than completing the slideshow all in one night.

This wasn’t necessarily a “sweats and tears” project, contrary to my other projects that actually required coding, but it’s definitely a project that I find myself more drawn and impressed towards. In order to create this work, I used my creativity and dedication (desire to put 100% in every responsibility & project) to make this as appealing and aesthetically-themed as possible, but I also used my knowledge in “what is a universal problem that can appeal to people of all ages, including myself?” and “how can I use my problem-solving skills to find a solution?”.
